iPad Air Rumored to Get OLED Display in 2027

April 16, 2026 · 2 min read

By Morgan Vale · Tech Editor

Apple's iPad Air might finally make the jump to OLED displays in 2027, according to supply chain reports from Display Supply Chain Consultants. If true, it would mark a significant upgrade for the mid-tier iPad lineup — and potentially signal the end of LCD iPads entirely.

Why OLED Matters

OLED screens offer true blacks, infinite contrast ratios, and Apple's AI photo featuresbetter power efficiency compared to LCD. Colors pop more, black backgrounds actually look black, and AI coding appsthe screen can be thinner since OLEDs don't need a backlight layer.

The iPad Pro already made the switch to OLED in 2024 (with the M4 iPad Pro), and it's widely considered the best tablet display Apple has ever made. Bringing that tech to the Air would be a major win for people who want Pro-level quality without the Pro price.

Wait — Isn't This Just for Pros?

👍 The Air would still be different from the Pro: Leakers suggest Apple's OLED Air wouldn't be the same "tandem OLED" stack used in the Pro. The Pro uses two OLED layers stacked for extra brightness. The Air would likely use a single OLED layer — still a massive upgrade over LCD, but not quite as bright as the Pro.

👎 Price is the big question: OLED iPad Pro starts at $999 for the 11-inch model. If an OLED iPad Air comes in significantly cheaper, it becomes a no-brainer. But if Apple prices it close to the Pro, buyers might as well spend a bit more for the better display stack.

What This Means for Current iPad Buyers

If you're buying an iPad right now, the current LCD iPad Air (M3 chip, $599) is still a great device. But if you can wait — and you care about display quality — 2027 might be the year to upgrade. OLED makes a real difference for reading, watching videos, and even just browsing. It's one of those improvements you don't fully appreciate until you've used it side-by-side with an LCD.
