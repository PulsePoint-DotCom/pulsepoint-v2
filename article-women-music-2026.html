Billboard's Women in Music 2026 List Just Dropped — Here's Who Made It

April 19, 2026 · 4 min read

By Aria Santos · Music Editor

Billboard dropped their annual Women in Music list today, and it's got everyone talking. This year's list highlights female artists who are not just dominating charts but shaping the conversation around what music can be in 2026. And honestly? This might be the most stacked list in years.

Headlining This Year

The top spots went to artists who've been on absolute fire since last year. Olivia Rodrigo continues her run as one of pop's most compelling voices — her new single "Drop Dead" debuted at Coachella last week with a surprise guest appearance from Addison Rae that nobody saw coming but everyone loved. The performance broke streaming records within hours.

Sabrina Carpenter also gets a major highlight after her Coachella moment where she brought out Madonna for Weekend Two — a generational collab that sent Twitter into meltdown. Add in TAEMIN making history as the first K-Pop male artist to appear on this list (breaking the binary categorization debate that's been brewing for years), and you've got a list that reflects where music actually is right now.

The K-Pop Moment

🎤 TAEMIN's take: In his interview for the list, TAEMIN called second-generation K-Pop "the generation of dreams coming true" — referring to how artists like himself are finally getting recognition outside traditional categories. He's right. The lines between genres and markets are blurring faster than ever, and K-Pop has been the bridge.

Zara Larsson also appears prominently, with her "Midnight Sun" world tour generating some of the biggest live music numbers of the year so far.

Why This List Matters

Billboard's Women in Music list has its critics — some argue it's reductive to separate by gender in 2026. But the list's proponents point out that representation still matters when looking at festival lineups, award show inclusions, and major label investment priorities. The hope is that lists like this become less necessary over time. Until then, they serve as a real snapshot of who's actually dominating.

If you're looking for new music to explore, this list is a solid starting point. Every artist on it earned it.
