Summer Olympics 2026: The Athletes, Events, and Stories You Need to Watch

July 20, 2026 · 5 min read

The Summer Olympics are in full swing, and if you're not paying attention yet, now's the time to start. This year's Games have already delivered some of the most unexpected, thrilling, and heartbreaking moments in recent Olympic memory — and we're only halfway through.

From Chloe Kim's emotional run to a teenage snowboarding phenom stealing the spotlight (yes, even in summer — winter sports have their own calendar), the Games have not lacked for storylines. Here's your guide to what's mattered most so far and what's coming up.

The Headlines That Stuck

Chloe Kim — one of the most recognizable athletes in American sports — competed in what many believe may have been her final Olympic appearance. She fell just short of the podium in her signature event, but the reaction from fans and fellow athletes alike showed just how much she means to the sport. In the same event, a 17-year-old from South Korea known as "Force" put down a run that social media immediately dubbed the "score of the century" — and the debate about judging began almost before she crossed the finish line.

Quick read: Snowboarding's new generation just arrived. Whether that's exciting or concerning depends on whether you're a fan of the old guard.

What's Coming Up

Track and field kicks into high gear this week, with the men's 100m final expected to draw one of the largest Olympic TV audiences in years. A new crop of sprinters from Jamaica, the USA, and Great Britain have been breaking personal records all season — and the final is anyone's race.

Swimming has also delivered surprises, with several long-standing world records falling in the first week — a sign that the post-pandemic training cycle may finally be producing faster, stronger competition across the board.

How to Watch

NBC has partnered with several streaming platforms to offer wall-to-wall coverage, but if you want the fastest updates, social media remains the real-time scoreboard. Just be careful where you're getting your info — Olympic-related misinformation spikes every Games, from fake results to impersonated athletes.

The Closing Ceremony is scheduled for August 8th. Here's to a memorable second half of the Games.
