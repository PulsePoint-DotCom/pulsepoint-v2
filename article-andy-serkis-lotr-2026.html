Andy Serkis Defends Lord of the Rings Casting — and the Internet Has Thoughts

July 14, 2026 · 4 min read

Andy Serkis — the man who brought Gollum to life — is speaking out about the casting choices in the upcoming Lord of the Rings films, and he's not apologizing for them. The new films, which have already cast Kate Winslet, Jamie Dornan, and Anya Taylor-Joy in major roles, have faced scrutiny over diversity in casting — and Serkis is pushing back hard.

"The world of Middle-earth has always been diverse," Serkis reportedly said in a recent interview. "People forget that Tolkien wrote about a world that spanned continents. These stories belong to everyone." It's a familiar argument — and one that's become increasingly common in franchise filmmaking — but Serkis brings a credibility that most studio PR teams can't manufacture. He's been in the trenches of what people consider "authentic" LOTR casting, and he's choosing to lead this particular charge.

The Cast So Far

The new films have assembled a genuinely star-studded cast. Winslet plays the role of Galadriel — a casting choice that's been widely praised for the weight and intensity she brings. Dornan and Taylor-Joy round out a lineup that's generating real excitement, despite the controversy. For every person upset about casting choices, there are five who are just happy new LOTR films exist.

Whether Serkis's defense will quiet the discourse remains to be seen — but having someone of his stature willing to lean into the conversation rather than avoid it is notable. The films are expected in 2027, and the conversation around them isn't slowing down.
