NBA Playoffs 2026 Are Here — And the First Round Is Absolutely Wild

April 25, 2026 · 5 min read

The NBA Playoffs are back, and if you haven't been paying attention, Game 1 of the Lakers vs Rockets series just delivered everything basketball fans could want. LeBron James is still doing LeBron James things, the young Rockets squad isn't backing down, and the first round is already delivering iconic moments.

Meanwhile, the Celtics are battling the Knicks in what many are calling the most anticipated first-round matchup in years. The classic franchise rivalry — Boston's veteran core versus New York's hungry young squad led by a breakout star — has the league buzzing. If you haven't watched yet, this weekend is the time to start.

Lakers Steal Game 1 Against Rockets

The Lakers didn't just win Game 1 — they made a statement. LeBron put on a vintage performance with a near triple-double, and the supporting cast stepped up when it mattered most. The Rockets, a young team that surprised everyone this season with their aggressive playstyle, fought hard but couldn't close it out at the end.

For XC runners and athletes who appreciate the mental side of competition, this series is a masterclass in experience versus energy. The Lakers know how to manage a playoff game; the Rockets are learning on the fly. That contrast makes every possession feel meaningful.

What Else to Watch

Beyond Lakers-Rockets and Celtics-Knicks, the Western Conference has multiple compelling matchups. The defending champions are in the mix, and the field is deep enough that any of five teams could realistically raise the trophy in June.

On the hockey side, the NHL Stanley Cup Playoffs are also underway with their own set of intense matchups. If you're a sports fan who loves the grind of a long playoff run, April and May are the best months on the calendar.

How to Watch

ESPN has full coverage of all series. You can catch games live on ESPN, ABC, and their streaming platform. If you're planning to follow multiple series, the ESPN app is your best bet for real-time scores and highlights. And if you're in a fantasy league — whether it's basketball, baseball, or hockey — now is the most critical stretch of your season.

Bottom line: if you've been putting off getting into the playoffs, now is the moment. The storylines are real, the stakes are high, and the next few weeks will produce moments you'll be talking about for years.
