The Summer Memes That Actually Broke the Internet in 2026

July 20, 2026 · 3 min read

The internet doesn't slow down for summer — it just changes clothes. If you've been offline for a few weeks and are now hopelessly confused when you open Twitter or TikTok, here's your primer on the memes that actually landed this July.

"It's giving…" — The Meme That Won't Quit

You can't escape it. "It's giving…" started as a fashion critique format on TikTok ("It's giving… poverty," "It's giving… main character energy") and has mutated into a universal way to express vibe-checking anything and everything. It's been applied to world leaders, neighborhood squirrels, airport Wi-Fi, and the concept of Monday itself. The format is so versatile that it's still generating millions of views per day, months after its peak.

The "NPC Streaming" Phenomenon

Remember when Twitch streamers started behaving like video game NPCs — repeating the same phrases, walking the same paths, responding to donations with scripted dialogue? It's back, but this summer it's evolved. Now it's AI-powered NPCs that learn from chat and slowly develop personalities over live streams. The creepiest part: some of them have started going "off-script" in ways their creators can't explain. Internet sleuths are having a field day.

That One Olympic Clip

Without spoiling results, there's one Olympic moment from this year's Games that has been clipped, reposted, remixed, slowed down, and soundtrack-applied more times than any other piece of footage so far. We'll let you find it — but if you've seen the phrase "how did they even DO that" more than once this week, you know exactly what we're talking about. It's already been turned into a puzzle game, a meditation app notification, and a surprisingly emotional ASMR video.

The "Old Enough to Know Better" Trend

Gen Z and millennials have found common ground in posting things they did as kids that they absolutely should have known better about — but did anyway. The format is a photo from the early 2010s of something reckless, embarrassing, or deeply regrettable, captioned "old enough to know better in 2026." It's nostalgic, slightly self-deprecating, and has become a weekly ritual on Fridays.

Summer's only halfway over. There's still time for something completely unexpected to take over. That's usually how the best memes work — you never see them coming.
