Lakers Steal Game 1 Against Rockets as LeBron Puts On a Show

April 19, 2026 · 4 min read

By Marcus Chen · Sports Editor

LeBron James isn't showing any signs of slowing down. In Game 1 of the Western Conference playoffs, LeBron and Luke Kennard hit back-to-back clutch threes in the final minutes to lift the Lakers over the Rockets 114-112, stealing home court advantage in a series that was supposed to be a lot closer.

The Moment That Won It

With under two minutes left and the Lakers down by two, LeBron buried a contested three from the wing. Kennard followed immediately with another from beyond the arc on the next possession. That's a 6-0 swing in under 30 seconds. The Rockets never recovered.

LeBron's Historic Night

🏀 By the numbers: LeBron finished with 28 points, 10 assists, and 7 rebounds. But the eye test was even more impressive — he was attacking the basket with authority, hitting off-balance floaters, and making passes that made defenders look silly. His 8 assists in the first quarter alone had ESPN commentators scrambling for historical comparisons.

The Rockets came in as the higher seed and had dominated the regular season series. But playoff basketball is different, and LeBron knows it better than anyone. This is his 22nd (!) postseason, and it shows. He's not just scoring — he's controlling the tempo, getting everyone involved, and making the right play almost every single time down the court.

What This Means for the Series

The Lakers stealing Game 1 on the road changes everything. The Rockets now have to win in Los Angeles just to tie the series, and history says LeBron in a tied series with a chance to push ahead? That's bad news for whoever's across from him. If you're a basketball fan, this series is appointment viewing from here on out.

The Nuggets also won their Game 1, so the West semifinals are shaping up to be a potential Lakers-Nuggets rematch. And honestly? That's the storyline everyone wanted.
