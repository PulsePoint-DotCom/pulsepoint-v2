The Cozy Games Taking Over Summer 2026

July 20, 2026 · 4 min read

Summer is supposed to be relaxing. But according to gaming data from the first half of 2026, more people than ever are choosing their vacation days to be… well, virtual. The cozy games genre — games built around low stakes, calming aesthetics, and gentle progression — has exploded into one of the fastest-growing categories in gaming.

And this summer, there's a new crop of titles competing for your attention.

What's Driving the Trend

Cozy games aren't new — Stardew Valley came out in 2016 and basically invented the modern version of the genre. But 2026 has seen a wave of high-profile releases and a notable shift in who is playing them. Gen Z and younger millennials, many of whom grew up on fast-paced competitive games, are increasingly turning to cozy titles as a form of digital self-care. The data backs it up: searches for "cozy games" on gaming forums have tripled since 2024.

Games to Know Right Now

Wanderhome 2 — The sequel to the beloved indie hit about exploring a pastoral world with animal companions. The sequel adds multiplayer up to four players, making it a go-to for friend groups who want something relaxing to play together. It's been sitting in the top 20 of Steam's bestsellers for six consecutive weeks.

Bonfire Bites — A cooking and camping sim where you manage a mobile food truck at national parks across the US. Think Overcooked meets Parks and Rec. The gameplay loop is simple but oddly addictive, and the art style — watercolor-inspired backgrounds — has made it a favorite on TikTok.

Hollow Cove — A mystery-solving game set in a small seaside town. You play as a librarian who inherits a mysterious estate and discovers it's haunted — but the ghosts are more mischievous than menacing. It's been described as "a cozy mystery" and has one of the strongest written narratives in the genre this year.

Why This Matters for Gaming

The success of cozy games is reshaping how publishers think about game design. AAA studios are taking notice — Nintendo's continued support of the Animal Crossing franchise and Sony's recent acquisition of a cozy game studio signal that the genre is too big to ignore. Meanwhile, indie developers continue to lead the space with the most innovative and heartfelt titles.

Whether you're looking to unwind after a long day or planning a gaming marathon with friends, summer 2026 might be the best time yet to go cozy.
